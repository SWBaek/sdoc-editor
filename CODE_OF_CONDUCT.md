# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Structured Doc Editor a respectful and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or role.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, constructive, and specific when discussing ideas or code.
- Assume good intent while addressing technical and social impact directly.
- Welcome questions and help contributors understand project constraints.
- Accept feedback gracefully and focus criticism on the work, not the person.
- Respect privacy, attribution, licenses, and organizational confidentiality.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized language, or unwanted attention
- Personal attacks, insults, trolling, or sustained disruption
- Publishing another person's private information without permission
- Knowingly submitting material that violates copyright, license, employment, or confidentiality obligations
- Retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove comments, commits, issues, and other contributions that violate this code. They may issue a warning, temporarily limit participation, or permanently ban a participant when necessary and will seek a proportionate, consistent response.

For an ordinary moderation concern, contact the repository maintainer through their GitHub profile or an issue that does not expose private details. For a sensitive report that cannot be made publicly, use the repository's [private security advisory form](https://github.com/SWBaek/sdoc-editor/security/advisories/new) and state clearly that it is a conduct report. Reports will be handled as privately as practical; conflicts of interest should be disclosed.

This code applies in project spaces and when someone officially represents the project in public spaces.
